32. 10 nama binatangbahasaInggris beserta berkembang biak,cara bertahan hidup, ciri cirinya​


Jawaban:

1. frog

how to breed : In breeding, frogs use external fertilization. The female frog will release a collection of eggs in the water. Then the male will release sperm in the water to fertilize these eggs. A fertilized egg will develop in the water until it hatches, and not in the female's body.

how to survive: Adult frogs shed their tails and have a perfect shape. Frogs will spend most of their life on land and breathe using their lungs. The frogs will return to the water for reproduction.

characteristics:

-Young frogs live in water and breathe with gills.

-Adult frogs live on land and breathe using their lungs.

-Frogs reproduce by laying eggs.

2. cat

how to breed: The sperma from the male cat meets the female cat's ovum to form a zygote.

The zygote grows and develops in the uterus of the female cat into an embryo during pregnancy.

how to survive: Cats have sharp claws to protect themselves.

Cats have eyes that can glow green in the dark and can see more clearly in the dark.

characteristics:

-have claws

-the ur is soft

3. elephant

how to breed:  Elephants reproduce by giving birth (viviparous). Elephant mothers carry their children for approximately 22 months.

how to survive: has a large body that makes some animals afraid

characteristics:

-have a trunk

-has a large body

4. ant

how to breed: The Queen does indeed carry eggs to become young ants, but the most important role is the drones who have the task of mating with the queen. In addition to being males, these eggs usually hatch into winged females who will become queens in an ant colony

how to survive: Ants can survive through cooperating activities in gathering food, fighting intruders, and helping each other.

Elephants are huge animals. They mostly live in Africa and some elephants live in Asia. Their hard skin is of grey colour. Elephants have proboscis, used to grab their food, drink, or grab other objects. Another outstanding characteristic of an elephant is their two tusks, used to move objects, fight other elephants to win a territory, and to dig in the floor. Elephants are an endangered species, because people hunt them for their tusks to sell on the market.

When elephants mate, the male and female might rub their tusks against each other. Sometimes, the female runs away from the male, and it might take some time before they actually start the mating. After a female elephant has brought a young to life, they make sure that the young is protected and not harmed from anything that could harm them. When the herd is on the move, the young elephants follow their mother. The female elephant usually keeps the young between her tusks to ensure its safety.

In zoos, the workers try to mate elephants to increase the amount of elephants in the world. Because elephants keep getting hunted, this is very necessary to prevent the extinction of this large animal.
