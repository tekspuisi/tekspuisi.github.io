4. The Little Rose Treeby Rachel FieldEvery rose on the little tree Is making a different face at me! Some look surprised when I pass by, And others droop—but they are shyThese two whose heads together press Tell secrets I could never guessSome have their heads thrown back to sing, And all the buds are listening. I wonder if the gardener knows, Or if he calls each just a rose?Answer the questions based on the poem above:1. Can roses speak to each other?2. Can roses sing a song?3. Does a rose have a face like a human?4. Why does the writer describe the roses like humans?5. What do the roses do in this poem? They makes faces, ....... ....... and they even .....6. What figure of speech does the writer use in her poem?


Answer the questions based on the poem above:

1. Can roses speak to each other?

Answer: Yes, they can. The sixth line say "Tell secrets I could never guess." From this line we can summarize that the roses can speak.

2. Can roses sing a song?

Answer: Yes, they can. From the line, "Some have their heads thrown back to sing and all the buds are listening," we can summarize that the roses can sing.

3. Does a rose have a face like a human?

Answer: Yes, it does. From the first line, 'every rose on the little tree is making a different face at me.'

4. Why does the writer describe the roses like humans?

Answer: The writer describes the roses like humans so the reader can have a better understanding in nature and their actions. It makes the description more vivid and clear.

5. What do the roses do in this poem? They make faces, ....... ....... and they even .....

Answer:  The roses can make faces, talk/speak, sing and they even listen.

6. What figure of speech does the writer use in her poem?

Answer: Figurative language that uses in this poem is personification.

Penjelasan

Figurative language adalah kalimat yang menggunakan kata-kata kiasan atau bukan makna yang sesungguhnya. Dalam bahasa Indonesia sehari-hari biasanya disebut bahasa kiasan atau majas.

Figurative language yang digunakan dalam puisi diatas adalah personifikasi (personification) yaitu bahasa figurative yang memberikan sifat-sifat benda hidup pada benda mati.  Contoh figurative language dalam puisi diatas antara lain:

Every rose on the little tree  

Is making a different face at me!  

Some look surprised when I pass by,  

And others droop—but they are shy

These two whose heads together press  

Tell secrets I could never guess

Some have their heads thrown back to sing,  

And all the buds are listening.  

I wonder if the gardener knows,  

Or if he calls each just a rose?

Dalam puisi diatas, roses diberikan sifat-sifat benda hidup dan memiliki ekspresi seperti surprised, shy, bisa berbicara (can talk), bisa bernyanyi (can sing) dan bisa mendengar (can listen).

Jawaban:

Lukisan "The Soul of the Rose" (Jiwa Mawar) merupakan salah satu lukisan terkenal yang diciptakan oleh pelukis Inggris, John William Waterhouse. Lukisan ini menggambarkan seorang wanita muda dengan gaun putih yang sedang duduk di tengah-tengah kebun mawar dengan sejumlah bunga mawar di sekitarnya.

Makna dari lukisan ini dapat terbuka untuk interpretasi yang berbeda-beda, tergantung pada sudut pandang setiap individu. Namun, ada beberapa tema yang sering kali dihubungkan dengan lukisan ini.

1. Kecantikan dan Kebahagiaan Perempuan: Lukisan ini mungkin menggambarkan kecantikan alami dan kebahagiaan perempuan muda. Gaun putih yang dikenakan oleh wanita tersebut sering kali dikaitkan dengan kepolosan dan keanggunan.

2. Simbolisme Mawar: Mawar sering kali melambangkan keindahan, romansa, dan cinta. Dalam lukisan ini, mawar-mawar di sekitar wanita tersebut dapat melambangkan kehadiran asmara atau romantisme yang terkandung dalam jiwa sang wanita.

3. Sentuhan Romantis: Lukisan ini menggambarkan suasana yang lembut dan romantis. Komposisi warna yang lembut dan pemandangan yang indah dapat menggambarkan perasaan dan suasana hati yang romantik.

4. Simbolisme Jiwa: Judul lukisan ini, "The Soul of the Rose," mungkin mengindikasikan adanya representasi jiwa dalam lukisan tersebut. Ada kemungkinan bahwa gambaran wanita muda dalam lukisan ini melambangkan keanggunan dan kelembutan jiwa.

Namun, penting untuk diingat bahwa interpretasi seni subjektif dan dapat berbeda antara individu. Anda mungkin memiliki pandangan dan interpretasi yang berbeda tentang lukisan ini berdasarkan pengalaman dan persepsi pribadi Anda.

Some look surprised when I pass by And others droop-but they are shy These two whose heads together press Tell secrets I could never guess Some have their heads thrown back to sing, And all the buds are listening I wonder if the gardener knows Or if he calls each just a rose A. FIGURE of SPEECH - PERSONIFICATION  

A. Why does the writer describe the roses like humans?  

The writer describes the roses like humans to make the readers relate to the roses more easily.

B. Can roses sing a song?  

Literally, they can't, but here, figuratively, they can.

 ⟱

Some have their heads thrown back to sing

C. Can roses speak to each other?

Literally, they can't, but here, figuratively, they can.

 ⟱

Tell secrets I could never guess  

-----------------

EXPLANATION

Personification is a figure of speech often employed in literary works such as poetry and in also everyday speech.

Personification is giving human attributes to non-human objects.

The objects are personified so that we (the readers or listeners) can understand their nature and actions better.

We, as humans, tend to see things from our human perspective, so it is easier for us to relate to something that has human traits.

Personification also makes the texts more vivid and give them deeper meanings.

1. Every rose on the little trees Is making a different face  

2. Some look surprised
